STDs

There are many STDs, but we have laid out some of the most common in hopes that you will take the initiative to learn more.

    HIV

  • How To Get It?

    • Spreads by sexual contact and also through contact with infected blood, semen, or vaginal fluids.

  • Symptoms?

    • There are flu-like symptoms such as fever, sore throat, and fatigue can occur. There can also be pain in the abdomen with a dry cough. Loss of appetite, opportunistic infection, headache, oral thrush, pneumonia, red blotches, severe unintentional weight loss, skin rash, or swollen lymph nodes, malaise, night sweats, or sweating is also common.

  • Prevention?

    • Practicing safe sex is the best prevention method.

  • Treatment?

    • There is HIV medication that is meant to slow down the virus.

    AIDS

  • How To Get It?

    • AIDS is a disease in which there is a severe loss of the body's cellular immunity, greatly lowering the resistance to infection and malignancy. AIDS can be spread through any type of unprotected sex (oral, vaginal, or anal).

  • Symptoms?

    • Symptoms include weight loss, fever or night sweats, fatigue, and recurrent infections.

  • Prevention?

    • Practicing safe sex.

  • Treatment?

    • No cure exists for AIDS, but strict adherence to antiretroviral therapy (ART) can dramatically slow the disease progress, prevent secondary infections and complications, and prolong life.

    Syphilis

  • How To Get It?

    • Syphilis is a sexually transmitted infection that is divided into the different stages (primary, secondary, latent, and tertiary). These stages have different symptoms. This is transmitted with person to person contact by touching a syphilitic sore.

  • Symptoms?
    • Primary Stage:

    During this stage single or multiple sores. They usually appear where the contact happened. The sores are usually painless.

    Secondary Stage:

    During this stage the infected person may experience skin rashes or membrane lesions. The infected may also have can include fever, swollen lymph glands, sore throat, patchy hair loss, headaches, weight loss, muscle aches, and fatigue (feeling very tired).

    Latent Stage:

    This stage has no visible signs.

    Tertiary Stage

  • Prevention?

    • Practicing safe-sex is the best way to prevent syphilis.

  • Treatment?

    • Syphilis can be treated with penicillin. The treatment will not undo any damage that the infection already caused.

    HPV (Human Papillomavirus Infection)

  • How To Get It?

    • HPV is an infection that causes warts on various parts of the body. HPV is transmitted through skin-to-skin contact. That includes kissing and sex.

  • Symptoms?

    • Many people do not get symptoms before the warts appear. The warts are the only symptom that can be shown.

  • Prevention?

    • Can be prevented with safe-sex. There are also two FDA approved vaccinations that a person can get to help prevent HPV.

  • Treatment?

    • There is no ultimate cure for the virus, but the warts can go away with time and help like special creams.

    Hepatitis B

  • How To Get It?

    • Hepatits B is a serious infection to the liver that is caused by a virus. This can be transmitted with any contact with another infected person's bodily fluids. This includes any type of sex, but specifically anal sex.

  • Symptoms?

    • The symptoms include tiredness, loss of appetite, stomach discomfort and yellow skin.

  • Prevention?

    • Practicing safe-sex. There is also a vaccine that helps prevent people from getting it.

  • Treatment?

    • The virus can go away on its own and be treated with special medication.

    Crabs (Pubic Lice)

  • How To Get It?

    • Most often, they are transmitted via sexual contact, but it it possible to get them through sleeping in a bed where someone with crabs has slept, by using their towel, wearing their clothes, or even using a toilet seat after they've gone.

  • Symptoms?

    • The biggest indicator of crabs is the itching around the pubic region of the body (where your pubic hair is). You can also see the lice and nits (eggs) in your hair.

  • Prevention?

    • To keep crabs from spreading from person to person, someone who has crabs should tell all people that they've recently had sexual contact with to prevent further transmission, stop having sex until the crabs are gone, wash and dry your clothes at 130 degrees or store them in a sealed, airtight bag for two weeks.

  • Treatment?

    • People with crabs can get a lice-killing lotion and put it on the infected hair. However, if the person cannot use the lotion or has tried and still has crabs, they can get a prescription for Lindane, a lice- killing shampoo. To apply medication, someone should was the infected area, apply the medication, and then go through the hair with a fine toothed comb. Nits, which are attached to the root of the hair, will be removed after being brushed out. Refrain from having sex for 9-10 days to ensure the death of all crabs.

    Trichomoniasis

  • How To Get It?

    • Trichomoniasis is an extremely common STD (3.7 million people in America have this infection), caused by a protozoan parasite. It is transferred via sex, as places such as the cervix, urethra, vagina, and vulva. Any sort of unprotected sex between people can spread the parasite.

  • Symptoms?

    • Most people who are affected do not have any symptoms, however those who do often experience mild irritation to severe inflammation. Males who are infected experience itching inside the penis, burning after urination, and discharge from the penis. Females experience itching, burning, and redness in vagina, cervix, and urethra, uncomfortable urination, and a change in the vagina discharge ( such as in more discharge, less discharge, change in color of discharge, and an unusual fishy smell. Although this STD is not particularly dangerous, it allows for other STDs to infect the individual than previously.

  • Prevention?

    • Like any other STD, the only way to be completely safe is to abstain from sex (but let's be real that's not a viable option for most people). To help stay safe, it is best to use a latex condom. This method cannot protect you completely, as this STD affects other regions outside of the condom.

  • Treatment?

    • The only way to treat this STD is to go to a doctor and get a subscription for metronidazole or tinidazole, which is an oral pill. They take 7-10 days to take effect, so those who are infected should abstain from sex until they are sure that they no longer carry the parasite and reach out to their sexual partners and make sure that they get tested or treated.

    Herpes

  • How To Get It?

    • It is spread by mother to baby by pregnancy, labor, or nursing, or by having unprotected vaginal, anal, or oral sex.

  • Symptoms?

    • Pain, itching, and small sores appear first. They form ulcers and scabs. After initial infection, genital herpes lies dormant in the body. Symptoms can recur for years. People may experience pain in the penis or vagine and can occur during urination.

  • Prevention?

    • Practice Safe Sex!

  • Treatment?

    • Medications can be used to manage outbreaks. It's very common with more than three million US cases per year. Treatment can help, but this condition can't be cured. It can last for years or even lifetime.

    Gonorrhea

  • How To Get It?

    • It spreads from mother to baby by pregnancy, labor, or nursing, or by having unprotected vaginal, anal, or oral sex.

  • Symptoms?

    • Symptoms include painful urination and abnormal discharge from the penis or vagina. Men may experience testicular pain and women may experience pain in the lower belly. In some cases, gonorrhea has no symptoms.

  • Prevention?

    • Practicing safe sex is important, but also Regular screening can help detect instances when an infection is present despite having no symptoms.

  • Treatment?

    • Gonorrhea can be treated with antibiotics such as ceftriaxone, azithromycin, and doxycycline.

    Chlamydia

  • How To Get It?

    • Chlamydia is a bacterial infection. The bacteria are usually spread through sex or contact with infected genital fluids (semen or vaginal fluid). You can get chlamydia through: unprotected vaginal, anal or oral sex.

  • Symptoms?

    • Many who have chlamydia don't develop symptoms, but they can still infect others through sexual contact. Symptoms may include genital pain and discharge from the vagina or penis.

  • Prevention?

    • The best prevention menthod is practicing safe sex.

  • Treatment?

    • Antibiotic therapy for the affected patient and the patient's sexual partners is recommended. Screening for other common sexually transmitted infections should also be performed.
